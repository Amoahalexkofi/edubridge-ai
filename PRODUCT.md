# Product

## Register

product

## Users

EduBridge AI serves four roles in Ghana's exam-prep ecosystem:

- **Students (primary)** — JHS candidates preparing for the BECE and SHS candidates preparing for the WASSCE, roughly ages 14–18. They are predominantly on **mobile phones, on metered/low-bandwidth data**, often studying on shared devices and outside school hours. Their job: understand topics, practise, sit realistic mock exams, and know exactly what to improve.
- **Teachers** — author lessons and questions, and monitor who is falling behind.
- **Parents/Guardians** — a read-only window into their ward's progress and exam readiness; many are not tech-savvy.
- **Administrators** — manage users, roles, content and platform analytics.

## Product Purpose

Affordable, curriculum-aligned BECE & WASSCE preparation in one mobile-first platform: lessons, practice questions, timed mock exams with auto-marking and per-topic feedback, a 24/7 AI tutor, and progress analytics. It exists because quality exam prep in Ghana is expensive, uneven and city-concentrated. Success = students signing up, completing lessons, taking mock exams, and measurably improving — with parents and teachers able to follow along.

## Brand Personality

**Trustworthy, encouraging, clear.** The voice is a calm, capable mentor: confidence-building for students under real exam pressure, never patronising. Plain language over jargon. Every screen should reduce anxiety and make the next action obvious. Earns credibility (this is high-stakes) while staying warm and motivating.

## Anti-references

- **Generic AI/SaaS template look** — no cream/sand body backgrounds, gradient text, identical icon-card grids, or tiny tracked uppercase eyebrows on every section. If it reads "AI-generated," it has failed.
- **Cluttered government-portal feel** — no dense, text-heavy, hard-to-navigate screens.
- **Childish/cartoonish** — playful is fine for younger students, but never toy-like in a way that undercuts exam credibility.

## Design Principles

1. **Mobile-first, data-light** — design for a phone on metered data first; every screen must be fast and usable on a small screen and a slow connection.
2. **Reduce exam anxiety** — calm hierarchy, one clear primary action per screen, supportive copy. The product should feel like a steady coach.
3. **Show progress, always** — students should constantly see how far they've come and what's next (completion, scores, weak topics, streaks).
4. **Earn trust through clarity** — accurate, curriculum-aligned, honest. No inflated claims; legible, high-contrast, unambiguous.
5. **Role-appropriate focus** — each role sees exactly what serves its job and nothing that doesn't.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

- **WCAG 2.1 AA** target: body text ≥ 4.5:1 contrast, large text ≥ 3:1, visible focus states, full keyboard navigation, proper labels/alt text.
- **Mobile-first & low-bandwidth**: works well on small screens and slow/metered connections; tap targets ≥ 44px.
- **Reduced motion**: every animation has a `prefers-reduced-motion` alternative.
- Designed to be usable by non-technical parents and by students with varied digital literacy.
